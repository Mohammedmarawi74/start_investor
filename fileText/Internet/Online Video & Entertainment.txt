Online Video & Entertainment

With over 4.5 billion internet users worldwide, engaging in online videos and entertainment remains one of the most popular activities among users. Modern internet generations are increasingly attracted to visual content. Both long and short videos are getting millions, sometimes billions of views.

From the children’s song “Baby Shark”, to the reggaeton hit “Despacito”, and Korean singer Psy’s “Gangnam Style”, YouTube has seen several records in recent years. Apart from entertainment purposes, online videos offer tutorials, educational content, presentations, product reviews, and vlogs.

Although online video and entertainment are popular among all generations, young people tend to spend hours every week on viewing digital content. In 2023, global users spent over 28 hours engaging with the YouTube mobile app, with users in South Korea spending around 44.5 hours per month on the popular video platform via mobile. Leading platforms such as YouTube and Vimeo attract a growing number of users.

Leaders within the online video and entertainment industry include YouTube and TikTok., as well as over-the-top (OTT) video platform Netflix. YouTube’s audience can choose between a free version with advertisements and an ad-free premium subscription. By contrast, TikTok is focused on shorter videos and is free for the user. Advertising is the leading monetization method adopted by social video platform, while Netflix added ad-plans in 2023.

Online video entertainment has become especially popular among children. Easily accessed and re-clicked, some YouTube videos have generated millions of views. ChuChu TV Nursery Rhymes & Kids Songs has over 70 million subscribers and is by far, the most subscribed to YouTube channel for children. In the U.S. market, YouTube, Netflix, and Disney+ were the most popular video apps among kids.

Another platform that is especially popular among teenagers and young adults is TikTok. The number of daily active users peaked in the first quarter of 2020, after the COVID-19 pandemic broke out and people were spending more time at home, and in need of entertainment. With social media challenges, food content, dance videos, and influencer collaborations, TikTok attracts millions of users each day. Channels like Charli D’Amelio’s and Khaby Lame generate millions of views.
