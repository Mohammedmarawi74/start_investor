Sports & Fitness

Sports and fitness are not just vital for a nation's health, but also its economy. While professional sport makes substantial economic contributions through broadcasting, ticket sales and everything that comes with major public sporting events, amateur sports also play a key role, with the consumption of goods associated with sports - such as clothing, apparel, and equipment - representing a key industry in itself.

With the help of surveys and data from governing bodies, participation rates and membership numbers can be determined to get a picture of the individual sports profile of a country. As technology progresses, sports and fitness are affected by new markets, such as the digital health market, wearables such as smartwatches, and fitness apps.

Industry Definition

The sports and fitness industry covers trends and developments in different types of sports all around the world. This includes aspects such as sports participation, including which type of sport people take part in and how often and long they are active. The industry also covers trends in sports nutrition, and sports clothing and equipment. The fitness and health market, which has seen a steady rise in revenues and memberships in recent years, is also covered in the sports & fitness industry.

The industry also covers global sporting events such as the Olympic Games. Figures such as attendance, sponsorship values and broadcasting revenues provide insights for business opportunities and partnerships, as well as showing the popularity of sport across the world.
